✎ Punctuation
●
Full Stop  .
Use at the end of a statement sentence that is not a question or exclamation.
“The door creaked open.”   “She stepped inside.”
❓
Question Mark  ?
Use at the end of a direct question.
“Where did you go?”   “Was anyone watching?”
✗ Do NOT use for indirect questions: “She wondered where it had gone.”
❕
Exclamation Mark  !
Use to show strong emotion, surprise or a command. Use sparingly — one or two per piece.
“Run!”   “It was enormous!”
💬
Speech Marks  “ ”
Wrap around the exact words a character says. Start a new line for each new speaker.
““I found it”, she whispered.”
The punctuation goes inside the speech marks.
,
Comma  ,
Use to separate items in a list, or to join clauses with words like and, but, so, because.
“She grabbed her coat, her bag, and her torch.”
“He ran fast, but he was too late.”
'
Apostrophe  '
1. Possession — showing something belongs to someone
Add 's after the owner: “The detective's coat was torn.”
For plural owners ending in s, just add ': “The students' work.”
2. Contractions — letters left out
I'm = I am can't = can not don't = do not it's = it is won't = will not didn't = did not we're = we are they're = they are
✗ Don't confuse it's (it is) with its (belonging to it).

🔧 Writing Tool
📄 Improvement Steps
1
🤵 Develop Your Characters
When each main character is first introduced, add at least 2 sentences describing their physical appearance and personality. Use the Character tool on the left for ideas.
Tip: Show personality through action — don't just say “she was brave”, show her doing something brave.
2
💬 Add Dialogue
Add dialogue at three key points in the story. Mix action with speech — don't stack dialogue lines.
✗ Instead of: “I'm scared,” she said. “Me too,” he said. ✓ Try: She gripped his arm. “I'm scared.” He glanced over his shoulder. “Me too.” This makes the scene feel real.
3
👁 Descriptive Scenes
Choose 4 scenes from your story. In each scene, describe what the character sees, thinks, feels and does. These scenes should slow the story down and put the reader right inside the moment. Use the Describe tool on the left for vocabulary ideas.
4
🔞 Capital Letters
Read through your writing and check: ▪ Every sentence starts with a capital ▪ The word I is always a capital ▪ Names of people and places have capitals ▪ Days and months have capitals. Use the Capitals tool on the left to scan.
5
✎ Punctuation
Every sentence must end with a . ! or ? Check that speech marks “ ” open and close correctly around every piece of dialogue. Check for any missing commas in lists or long sentences. Use the Punctuation tool on the left.
6
✍ Spelling
Use the Check My Writing tool on the left to identify and fix spelling errors. Read your writing slowly, word by word.
7
💬 Replace all “Said”
Find every said in your writing and replace it with a word that shows how the character spoke — whispered, snapped, exclaimed, pleaded. Use the Said Words tool on the left.
8
📚 Overused Words
Use the Overused Words scanner on the left to find boring or repetitive words and replace them with more precise, interesting choices.
